Online Consent for Participation

This study is part of a research study conducted by Abhijat Biswas at Carnegie Mellon University.

Purpose
The purpose of the study is to evaluate and compare what vehicles and pedestrians do humans find important for safe driving in a driving scenario.

Procedures
You will be asked to view videos of a driving scenario and mark the vehicles you think are important in an image. This study may take up to 20 minutes.

Participation Requirements
Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older.

Risks
The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. There is a risk of breach of confidentiality, however We will decouple publicly released data from identifiable markers. The lookup table that will associate the public data with identifiable data will be encrypted and stored securely. It will be password protected and accessible only by team members.

Benefits
There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs
You will be compensated with $4 if you complete this study. There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study.

Future Use of Information and/or Bio-Specimens In the future, once we have removed all identifiable information from your data (information or bio-specimens), we may use the data for our future research studies, or we may distribute the data to other researchers for their research studies. We would do this without getting additional informed consent from you (or your legally authorized representative). Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be identified.

Confidentiality
The study will collect your research data through use of Amazon Mechanical Turk or Prolific. These companies are not owned by CMU. These companies will have access to your research data that you produce and any identifiable information that you share with them while using their product. Please note that Carnegie Mellon does not control the Terms and Conditions of the companies or how they will use or protect any information that they collect.
